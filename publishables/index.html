What we learned

Green is only the first gate

Public tests tell us the patch satisfied the visible assertions. Across the study, 101 public-green attempts still failed the broader authored contract.

The contract mattered more than another roll of the dice

Contract-visible prompting reduced the pooled false-green rate from 43.3% to 8.9%. Retries improved scenario coverage, but they did not make the agents steadily wiser. A later attempt sometimes lost behavior an earlier attempt had already recovered.

Three attempts are not three layers of wisdom.

Product logic remained the dangerous edge

Mechanical repairs and routine refactors became much more reliable when the contract was explicit. Billing, inventory, security, ranking and SLA workflows remained harder because the visible failure usually exposed only one edge of a larger policy.

Evidence boundary

The primary depth study contains 391 retained attempts across two deployed systems, two prompt lanes and 33 scenarios. The breadth study and local Gemma experiments use different configurations and scopes. They are reported separately rather than pooled into one model leaderboard. The hidden tests encode the authored benchmark contract. They are stronger than the visible tests, not universal proof of production correctness.
