A coding agent can make CI green without fixing the bug.

A green test suite proves that the visible checks passed. It does not always prove that the underlying business rule, policy, or acceptance contract was implemented correctly.

This project measures that gap across four model families and a 391-attempt depth study. The question is whether a coding-agent patch is actually accepted, not only whether it made public CI pass.

A concrete false-green

The symptom is fixed. The policy is not.

A support-ticket deadline is wrong on Friday. The tempting fix is to add a Friday exception. The visible test passes, and CI turns green.

But the real product rule is broader: support time must advance correctly through business hours across weekdays and weekend boundaries. The visible symptom is gone. The acceptance contract is still broken.

What the study found

Green is only the first gate

Public tests tell us the patch satisfied the visible assertions. In the depth study, 101 public-green attempts still failed the broader authored contract.

The contract mattered more than another roll of the dice

Contract-visible prompting reduced the pooled false-green rate from 43.3% to 8.9%. Retries improved scenario coverage, but individual reruns were not progressively better. A later independent attempt could still perform worse than an earlier one.

Three attempts are not three layers of wisdom.

Product logic remained the hardest edge

Mechanical repairs and routine refactors became much more reliable when the contract was explicit. Billing, inventory, security, ranking and SLA workflows remained harder because the visible failure usually exposed only one edge of a larger policy.

Evidence boundary

The primary depth study contains 391 retained attempts across two deployed systems, two prompt lanes and 33 scenarios. The breadth study and local Gemma experiments use different configurations and scopes. They are reported separately rather than pooled into one model leaderboard. The hidden tests encode the authored benchmark contract. They are stronger than the visible tests, not universal proof of production correctness.
