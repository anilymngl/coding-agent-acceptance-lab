Measuring Acceptance Reliability in Coding-Agent CI Repair

Two independently trained coding agents in a similar architecture class (3B-active MoE). Sparse product workflows produced the most persistent false-greens. Explicit contracts recovered most scenarios, but per-attempt reliability remained weaker—especially for North Mini. Laguna leads by 3 hidden passes overall, entirely in product_workflows.

Observed Both models converge to identical 9/12 hidden pass on ci_forensics sparse under any-of-observed aggregation. Two scenarios unconquered by both. Different third Achilles' heel per model.

Observed Contract exposure reduced the pooled attempt-level false-green rate from 43.3% to 8.9%. The remaining failures were concentrated in product workflows, particularly North Mini/OpenRouter. Every Laguna maintenance and product scenario had at least one successful contract-visible attempt.

Observed Product workflows sparse: 76.2% false-green attempt-level rate. 7 of 11 scenarios completely unconquered by both models at pass@3.

Observed Independent retries are not monotonically better — North Mini lost a solved scenario on its second attempt, and resampling from a wrong distribution can yield worse outcomes.

Observed Laguna leads by 3 scenario-cell successes overall (54/66 vs 51/66 scenario-cell successes under any-of-observed aggregation). The gap is entirely in product_workflows sparse (4 vs 2) and cv (11 vs 10).

Recommendation Partition CI repair by task type. Mechanical: delegate with clear specs. Business logic: human review.

Evidence Context

The canonical paper consolidates both stages of this research: Study A (five-model breadth study) testing 5 models at Pass@1 (~600 runs total), and Study B (depth study) running a Pass@3 head-to-head comparison between North Mini and Laguna XS.2 (391 attempts total). Historical snapshots are retained under `archive/` for provenance. See the main paper for details.
